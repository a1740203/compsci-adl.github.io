2019 Committee Nomination

Alexa Nguyen

2nd Year BCSA Student

Position(s)

  • Vice President
  • Secretary
  • Treasurer
  • General Committee

What will you bring to the CS Club?

My creativity, attention-to-detail, organisational skills, and initiative will allow me to thrive in any position on the committee. I am a committed and passionate individual with the foresight required to help the CS Club succeed and achieve its purpose and long-term goals. My aim is to create a supportive and welcome social environment for all CS students that will foster camaraderie, in addition to a friendly and trusting atmosphere amongst the committee members.

Why do you want to be on the committee?

I believe that being on the committee will allow me to contribute ideas that will help the club run more efficiently along with increasing student participation. I am also passionate about addressing the gender disparity in CS and hope to be able to develop strategies to attract, retain and promote women in the CS field by instilling in them a sense of confidence in technical skills as well as a sense of belonging within the community. Challenges with isolation and stereotyping still remain for women in CS where the number of women in higher-level CS courses decline dramatically and there is a lack of female representation among faculty and teaching assistants. By changing the microculture at university, I hope to be able to impact women’s participation in CS.

What previous experience do you have that will help you serve the club?

Having served on the Women in STEM Society Adelaide University (WISTEMS) as Publicity Officer in 2018, I oversaw the creation of the club’s online and on-campus marketing materials. During my time at WISTEMS, I also took the initiative to secure a generous donation for our quiz night from Adobe while I was volunteering at the BiG Day In, a one-day careers conference for high school students held at the University of Adelaide. Furthermore, I was recently elected to an executive position at Robogals Adelaide as Treasurer, where I manage the club’s financial direction for the year and will continue to develop crucial leadership skills. Lastly, my valuable experience as a CS tutor at university, and volunteer for Robogals, Code Like a Girl, and ACS, has enabled me to develop the communication and people skills to be able to effectively connect with people from diverse backgrounds.

Have you been on the CS Club committee before?

No
